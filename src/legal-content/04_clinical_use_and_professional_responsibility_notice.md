# AssessSuite Clinical Use and Professional Responsibility Notice

## 1. Who may use clinical functions

AssessSuite Clinical is initially intended for Australian practitioners with current Accredited Exercise Physiologist accreditation from Exercise & Sports Science Australia and for authorised practice staff acting within assigned roles. AEPs are self-regulated and ESSA-accredited; this notice does not describe them as Ahpra-registered practitioners.

An account or credential check does not establish competence for every patient, assessment, condition, program or jurisdiction. The practitioner must work within their individual scope, training, insurance and applicable professional and health-worker obligations.

## 2. Approved functions only

Clinical functions may be used only in the Approved Production Mode identified in the Customer Order Form and Approved Production Mode Schedule and the Regulatory Status and Funder Rules Schedule. Demo, synthetic-data, beta, disabled or unclassified functions must not be used for real patient care.

The following functions require specific regulatory and clinical release approval before production use:

- AI-generated interpretation or assessment recommendations;
- SOAP assessment or plan drafting;
- treatment protocols or exercise recommendations;
- medication considerations, contraindication or red-flag content;
- AI-assisted referral, funding or clinical reports;
- audio recording, transcription or consultation summarisation;
- prediction, prioritisation, diagnosis, monitoring or treatment determination.

## 3. The practitioner remains the decision-maker

The practitioner must independently:

- verify patient identity, referral, consent, source information and assessment conditions;
- decide whether a test or intervention is appropriate and safe;
- check calculations, norms, flags, contraindications, recommendations and citations;
- consider the patient’s history, medication, preferences, capacity, risks and alternatives;
- refer or escalate where the matter is outside scope or urgent;
- edit, approve and sign the final record or report;
- comply with applicable Medicare, DVA, NDIS, insurer, employer and professional requirements.

No output should be relied on merely because it appears polished, cites a source, follows SOAP format or resembles an official form.

## 4. AssessSuite’s own responsibilities

Professional review does not transfer all risk to the practitioner. AssessSuite remains responsible for:

- truthful intended-purpose and marketing statements;
- resolving therapeutic-goods classification before supply;
- reasonable privacy, security and product-safety controls;
- approved evidence, assessment-content and licence governance;
- model, prompt, source and version provenance;
- testing, monitoring, incident response, corrective action and rollback;
- making known limitations and material changes visible;
- preserving mandatory consumer and patient rights.

A disclaimer cannot convert a treatment recommendation into an administrative function or cure an insecure data flow.

## 5. AI and data limitations

AI output can omit facts, infer incorrectly, reproduce bias, use stale context, misapply a norm, fabricate a source or produce inconsistent answers. De-identification is not guaranteed where free text, audio, rare conditions or combinations of facts can identify a person.

Users must not enter real patient information into an unapproved AI function. When an AI function is approved, users must follow its collection notice, consent, input restrictions and human-sign-off controls.

## 6. Safety and escalation

AssessSuite is not an emergency service. A user must follow emergency and local escalation procedures for chest pain, severe breathlessness, syncope, neurological change, suicidality, abuse, acute deterioration, medication risk or another red flag.

Stop using an affected function and report promptly if:

- an output could cause or has caused harm;
- a recommendation is systematically unsafe or outside scope;
- a source, score, norm or report is wrong or unlicensed;
- information appears across tenants or to the wrong user;
- an AI output cannot be traced to its input and version;
- a patient’s consent, guardian authority or objection is unclear;
- an incident may require TGA, privacy, funder or other notification.

Reporting a concern does not prevent the user from meeting their own immediate patient-safety and regulator duties.

## 7. Consent and records

Clinical consent, privacy notice, recording consent, AI transparency and optional secondary-use consent are separate events. A digital signature records an event; it does not replace the conversation needed for informed consent.

The final record must distinguish source information, practitioner observations, system calculations, AI drafts, practitioner changes and approval. Corrections must preserve an audit trail.

## 8. Funding and program content

AssessSuite does not determine Medicare, DVA, NDIS or insurer entitlement and does not guarantee a claim, report or form is accepted. Scheme rules change. The practitioner must verify the effective rule, referral, item, provider number, session or cycle, form, evidence, fee and report obligation.

## 9. Regulatory status

AssessSuite’s workflow, EHR and established-questionnaire functions may be capable of separate exclusions from medical-device regulation, but the conclusion is feature-specific. AI or opaque clinical decision support cannot be assumed exempt. No user should infer ARTG inclusion, TGA approval or exemption unless the signed Intended Purpose, TGA Decision and Clinical Safety Case and the current Regulatory Status and Funder Rules Schedule state the exact basis and approved functions.

## 10. Acknowledgement

Before first clinical use and after a material change, the product should record that the user:

- holds the credential required for the enabled role;
- has read the current intended-use, limitations and AI notice;
- will independently review and sign clinical output;
- will obtain and record the required patient notice and consent;
- will report safety, privacy and security incidents promptly.

This acknowledgement must be versioned and separate from acceptance of the subscription contract.
