## 2. Information shown before purchase

Before payment or trial conversion, AssessSuite must show:

- the legal merchant and product;
- the exact plan, permitted users, features and material exclusions;
- the amount, currency and tax treatment;
- the billing period and first charge date;
- whether renewal is automatic and the next renewal date;
- any trial length, payment method requirement and conversion time;
- the cancellation method, effect and refund position;
- links to the current Terms, Privacy Policy and applicable schedules.

No free trial should be marketed until its duration, eligibility, feature limits, conversion event and reminder are configured and tested.

## 3. Renewal

1. Monthly and annual subscriptions renew for the same period only where automatic renewal was prominently disclosed and affirmatively accepted.
2. AssessSuite must implement the pre-contract, renewal and exit information required by law. As an additional voluntary standard unless final regulations prescribe otherwise, it will send an annual-renewal reminder at least 14 days before charge and a monthly receipt showing the next charge date.
3. A failed reminder does not remove statutory rights or permit a misleading renewal. The product must preserve the original acceptance and renewal notices.

## 4. Cancellation

1. Where sign-up is online, a Customer may cancel at any time through an online account pathway that is no harder than sign-up. The published support channel is an additional method, not a substitute.
2. Unless the Customer has another statutory or contractual termination right, cancellation stops renewal and access continues to the end of the paid billing period.
3. The cancellation confirmation must state the access-end date, export window, record-retention position and any action required before exit.
4. AssessSuite must not require a telephone call, new personal information, multiple retention screens or an undisclosed fee to cancel.

## 5. Refunds and credits

1. A change of mind does not automatically require a refund for a period already supplied, but this policy does not exclude Australian Consumer Law remedies.
2. If the Service has a major failure, is materially misrepresented, or cannot be supplied for its stated purpose, the Customer may have a right to cancel and obtain a refund or other remedy.
3. If AssessSuite makes a material adverse change to price, core scope, subprocessor risk, data location or Customer rights and the change is not required by law or urgent safety/security needs, the Customer may terminate before it takes effect and receive a pro-rata refund of prepaid fees for the unused affected period.
4. AssessSuite may give a proportionate service credit for material unplanned downtime or a failed paid feature where that is an appropriate remedy.
5. Refund decisions must be reasoned, recorded and made without relying on a blanket “no refunds” statement.

## 6. Price changes

AssessSuite must give at least 30 days’ prior notice of a price increase, or longer where the billing period or law reasonably requires. The notice must state the old and new price, effective renewal, cancellation method and any tax change. A price cannot be changed retrospectively for a period already paid.

## 7. Suspension, failed payments and data access

Before suspending for an ordinary failed payment, AssessSuite should give notice and a reasonable opportunity to correct it. Suspension must be proportionate and must not destroy clinical records or prevent an urgent lawful export, patient access request or incident response. Termination and deletion follow the **Customer-Facing Retention and Exit Schedule**, being Part A, sections 1–7, of the AssessSuite Records Retention, Export, Deletion and Exit Policy.

Billing and cancellation requests: admin@assesssuite.com or 1800 317 553. The product must generate a dated confirmation and preserve the request and outcome.
