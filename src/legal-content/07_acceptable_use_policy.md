## 1. Purpose

This policy protects patients, users, practices, AssessSuite and the Service. It applies to every Customer, authorised user, integration, upload and use of an AssessSuite environment.

## 2. Authorised use

You may use the Service only:

- for the Customer’s lawful professional and practice purposes;
- within the user’s assigned organisation, role, competence and credential;
- in the Approved Production Mode and in compliance with the Australian jurisdictional requirements applicable to the Customer and patient;
- with the notices, consents, referrals, licences and permissions required for the information and content;
- in accordance with the SaaS Terms, professional standards and applicable program rules.

Administrative users may support workflows but must not present themselves as the treating practitioner or approve clinical content unless independently authorised and qualified.

## 3. Prohibited clinical and high-impact use

You must not:

- use the Service as an emergency-response system;
- rely on an unreviewed output for diagnosis, treatment, referral, funding, eligibility, safety or another significant decision;
- use a test-only, synthetic-only, disabled or unclassified feature with real patient information;
- use an AI draft as a final record without source review, correction and practitioner sign-off;
- conceal AI involvement where the patient, recipient, program or law requires disclosure;
- practise outside scope, override a red flag without documented clinical reasons, or use the Service to avoid referral;
- represent a generated form as official, accepted or funder-approved unless the current schedule confirms that status;
- fabricate, backdate, alter provenance or submit a false clinical or funding record;
- use the Service for discriminatory profiling or a decision prohibited by law or professional duty.

## 4. Privacy and recording prohibitions

You must not:

- collect or upload more personal information than reasonably necessary;
- record or transcribe a person without the required express consent of every participant;
- bundle optional AI, recording, research, marketing or analytics consent with necessary care;
- use patient information for your own marketing, sale, model training or unrelated analytics without lawful authority;
- enter real patient information into a provider or environment that is not approved for it;
- use Medicare, DVA, NDIS or healthcare identifiers as general account identifiers;
- attempt to re-identify information described as de-identified;
- disclose a record to the wrong person, tenant, referrer, funder or representative.

## 5. Security prohibitions

You must not:

- share accounts, authentication codes or credentials;
- bypass access controls, impersonate another user or conceal the actor responsible for an action;
- access or test another practice, patient, user, object or tenant without written authority;
- upload malware, exploit code or content intended to disrupt or exfiltrate data;
- scrape or automate the Service in a way that creates harmful load, bypasses controls or accesses unauthorised data;
- conduct intrusive security testing outside the Vulnerability Disclosure Policy or unlawfully circumvent technical protections;
- use automated tools in a way that defeats rate limits, consent, human review or record integrity;
- store exported information on an insecure device or personal consumer service contrary to the practice’s policy.

Nothing in this policy excludes non-excludable rights concerning interoperability, error correction or security testing under the Copyright Act, an honest review, a protected disclosure or good-faith activity within the Vulnerability Disclosure Policy. AssessSuite will not penalise a person merely for reporting a concern responsibly.

## 6. Content and intellectual property

You must have authority to upload and use referrals, recordings, images, questionnaires, assessment tools, norms, protocols and other content. You must not remove attribution, reproduce or redistribute protected subscription content without authority, or upload material that infringes privacy, confidentiality, copyright, moral rights or contract restrictions. This does not prohibit lawful independent benchmarking, honest comparative review, interoperability work or reverse engineering to the extent permitted by non-excludable law or the Vulnerability Disclosure Policy.

## 7. Communications and conduct

You must not use the Service to harass, threaten, deceive, defame, exploit, discriminate, send unlawful marketing, or encourage unnecessary health services. You must not imply endorsement by ESSA, Medicare, DVA, NDIS, TGA or another body.

## 8. Incidents and cooperation

You must promptly report suspected tenant leakage, public file access, misdirected disclosure, credential compromise, unsafe output, adverse event, unlicensed content or other material breach. Preserve evidence, do not conceal the incident, and cooperate with proportionate containment and notification steps.

## 9. Enforcement

AssessSuite may restrict the affected account, record, integration or function where reasonably necessary for safety, security, legality, credentialing or material breach. We will use the least disruptive effective measure, give reasons and an opportunity to respond where practicable, and preserve lawful access to records and complaint rights.

Urgent restriction may occur without prior notice. It must be reviewed promptly. A restriction is not a substitute for fixing a platform defect or meeting AssessSuite’s own obligations.
