In this agreement:

- **Customer Data** means information supplied, created or controlled by Customer or its patients through the Service, excluding AssessSuite’s independently created account, billing, security and platform-administration data.
- **Customer-Facing Retention and Exit Schedule** means Part A (sections 1–7) of the AssessSuite Records Retention, Export, Deletion and Exit Policy, in the version and hash identified in the applicable Order Form or release manifest.
- **Liability Period** means each consecutive 12-month period beginning on the subscription start date recorded in the applicable Order Form or checkout transaction, with the final period ending when the agreement ends if it is shorter than 12 months.
- **Order Form** means either a Standard Online Order or a separately negotiated Customer Order Form and Approved Production Mode Schedule accepted by the parties for the subscription.
- **Patient Data** means Customer Data about a patient, client, representative, referrer or treating provider, including health information and clinical records.
- **Principal Party**, for an audio recording or transcription, means every person whose private conversation or identifiable voice may be captured, including the patient, practitioner, representative, interpreter, support person and remote participant.
- **Related Events** means events arising from the same or substantially the same originating cause, defect, instruction, incident or continuous course of conduct.
- **Service** means AssessSuite Clinical and only the functions and environment specified in the applicable Order Form.
- **Standard Online Order** means the electronic Order Form formed from: the owner account and practice profile; the plan, price, billing period, currency, tax treatment, renewal and cancellation particulars displayed at checkout; the successful checkout transaction; the owner’s mandatory document-bound acceptance receipts; and the then-current immutable production release manifest, feature configuration, provider schedule and incorporated policy versions and hashes. No separate signed document is required for that standard self-service order.
- **Subprocessor** means a provider engaged by AssessSuite to process personal information in supplying or supporting the Service; the label does not remove statutory duties imposed directly on AssessSuite.

## 3. Eligibility and authorised users

1. The initial clinical Service is for Australian exercise physiology practices and practitioners holding current Accredited Exercise Physiologist accreditation with Exercise & Sports Science Australia. AEP accreditation is not described as statutory registration.
2. Customer must ensure that:
   - each clinical user has the accreditation, provider number, insurance, training, first aid or other credential required for the function they perform;
   - administrative users act only within assigned roles and Customer governance;
   - credentials remain current and evidence is supplied when reasonably requested;
   - accounts are not shared and actions remain attributable to the person who performed them.
3. AssessSuite may restrict clinical functions until credentials are verified. Verification does not establish competence for every patient, task, jurisdiction or funding program.
4. Direct patient accounts, use outside Australia and use by another profession require a separately approved Order Form and product schedule. They are not authorised merely because an interface permits access.

1. Enabled automated functions and their limitations are described in the AssessSuite AI and Automated Processing Transparency Notice and the Order Form’s function schedule.
2. Recording and transcription remain disabled unless every principal party gives a separate time-stamped express-consent event before capture or transmission. A joining participant requires pause and new consent. The Service must capture participant and operator locations and apply the strictest potentially applicable Australian regime.
3. Customer must provide a non-recorded alternative without detriment where recording is optional. Withdrawal stops future capture but does not retrospectively alter authorised processing or a record that law requires to be retained.
4. Customer must not submit real Patient Data to a function labelled test-only, synthetic-only, disabled, de-identified-only or unapproved for the data and jurisdiction.
5. AssessSuite will not use identifiable Patient Data to train a general-purpose model or sell Patient Data. 7. Video, still-image and optical-surveillance capture are excluded from the initial Service and remain disabled pending a separate optical and workplace-surveillance legal schedule, Approved Production Mode and consent/control design.
